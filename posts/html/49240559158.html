_How we decide what to cover_
Picking which stories to run is often a battle of our voices because we are
all so passionate about each and every story we cover. Bottom line, we cover
what we think is inspirational. We are good at picking up trends early and
place a high value on design and communication. Our goal is that each day we
bring you something that makes you stop and think, or makes you want to look
into something a bit more, or incites you to purchase and support a cool
cause.

_Separation of Church and State_
We wholeheartedly believe in this and we do not ever pay for play. We only
feature what we believe in and receive no monetary benefit from any product,
company or cause that we cover. In saying that, we must pay our editorial
staff and others who help grow Milkshake. You will occasionally receive an
email that is marked “Dedicated” in the subject line and the email header.
These emails are paid for by our sponsors but mostly still written by us and
they promote whatever good the sponsor is doing in the world. Most companies
are doing good in some way and we are proud to feature them and encourage
you to support them.

_Vetting Causes_
There are so many causes in the world, some started by young kids, some that
have been around for many, many years. We research each and every cause
before running a story on it. We look to Charity Navigator and Guide Star
for accreditation.

_Milkshake Community_
What is most exciting to us is how Milkshake is catching on and the exposure
we are bringing to the many different products, people and places that are
doing good. That is why we started, to create a community of like-minded
people who are stimulated and excited by each story we bring them every day.
Of course some days are crazy and you just can’t get to all the emails that
you have to open, but the great thing about Milkshake is you can always go
back open and click through the dailies you miss and still find exciting
trips that give back, restaurants that are sustainable, upcoming designers
partnering with a cause, musicians raising money for disaster relief - we
could go on and on - but you get the point.
