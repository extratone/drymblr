“If you’re a woman living off tips, you’re practically guaranteed to
deal with sexual harassment. We’re tired of being told to accept it as
‘just part of the job.’ We are tired of feeling like standing up for
ourselves means putting our jobs on the line. We’re tired of letting
this giant industry get away with paying women a subminimum wage and
showing them their worth should be linked to enduring harassment from
customers, co-workers and managers. Enough is enough.” - Saru
Jayaraman and the ROC United Team

ONE BILLION RISING to Receive Compassion Week Award for Social Justice

Congratulations Risers! Tonight at the Fort Mason Center in San
Francisco, One Billion Rising will receive the inaugural
http://action.vday.org/site/R?i=XT36S_ElDjGNKer2uCsu_g

Compassion Week Award for Social Justice, presented by the Charter for
Compassion, the Empathy and Compassion in Society Conference and
sponsors GoodMojo and Tekmeca.

The Compassion Week 2014 Awards are presented in recognition of each
recipient’s work to make compassion and compassionate action a
transformative energy that motivates individuals and communities to
care for each other, relieve suffering wherever it is found, and reach
out to connect to other communities across the globe to ensure
well-being for all beings.

One Billion Rising joins notable organizations AIDS/LifeCycle,
Chinatown Community Development Center, Dignity Health, and Horizons
Foundation, and individual awardees Greg Fischer, Joan Blades, Joan
Brown Campbell, and Libby and Len Traubman.
