BREAKING NEWS: Why I am stepping down. (It’s not what you think.)
&

Dear Feminist for Life,

Like you, I have long understood the critical need for a national pro-woman,
pro-life advocacy and education organization which can empower women to make
choices that realize their goals and a bright future for their children.

I was delighted to learn that without known exception the first feminists were
pro-life, and that Feminists for Life members and supporters work to fulfill
Susan B. Anthony’s unrealized dream to address the root causes that drive women
to abortion.

I am proud of our message and materials, consistently amazed at the results of
our advocacy and educational programs–and I can see the tangible impacts of
our long-term strategies and initiatives.

I have seen how FFL’s message and solutions transform minds, campuses, political
parties, governments, and cultures.

And I am grateful to the other FFL Board members, our staff, volunteers, interns,
and you–our members and donors–who make our success possible, and who stand
with us to see our vital message amplified into coming generations.

Lastly, I am gratified to have led this organization’s Board of Directors. At
least I was until recently. There is one area where I know we have not measured
up–and I’m determined to change that.

And that is why I have decided to step down as Chair of FFL’s Board of Directors.

I am not leaving Feminists for Life; I intend to continue as an active member
and Board member, perhaps even more active than before. But I need to focus-with
your help on the one area where we need to grow: Our capacity to fund our
advocacy and education programs. In effect, what I’m really doing is “stepping
UP” to the task of helping to ensure a strong financial basis that will enable
FFL to have an even greater impact.

I have offered to go wherever I can help to find significant donors to enlarge
and step up the pace of our efforts on behalf of women and children.

My successor as FFL Board Chair, Cindy Pacewic Brown, is a proven leader and
a strategic thinker, and she will depend on us to help FFL move forward. We have
a dedicated Board, a too-small but talented staff, and terrific summer interns
who worked with staff on the Annual Capitol Hill Intern Briefing and many more
exciting projects.

FFL President Serrin Foster, the staff and editorial team has also been busy
working on the latest insightful issue of The American Feminist®.

As you know, our mission is to systematically address the root causes that drive
women to abortion, primarily a lack of resources and support.

Our new issue explores the opposite of support: Shame.

We explore the impact of shame as it relates to those who are pregnant or parenting
and poor, birthmothers, parents of large families, students, rape survivors and
those who were driven to abortion.

Our College Outreach Program coordinator is busy with the staff preparing for
the school year ahead.

Our forward-leaning focus and the need for preparation affords us only a brief
moment to celebrate the year just completed. Our speakers and staff traveled from
coast to coast bringing our powerful message of hope, declaring that “Women
Deserve Better® than Abortion” and that the post-Roe generation can “Refuse to
Choose®” between women and children, between sacrificing their education and
career plans and sacrificing their children.

Thanks to you and people like you, we had a terrific year, and plan to make this
summer and the 2014-2015 school year even better.

But it doesn’t happen without you and others who understand this requires planning,
strategy, marketing, printing, shipping, and guiding pro-life student leaders.

We look forward to reaching more Federal and state leaders with our “Pro-Woman
Answers to Pro-Choice Questions™” and other materials.

Because of Feminists for Life and your past support, every day we move closer
to creating a society that embraces life and addresses the root causes that
drive women to abortion.

Over the years I have personally witnessed the impact on students who hear FFL’s
message and solutions during lectures and Pregnancy Resource Forums at my alma
mater (UC Berkeley), Serrin’s debate at Harvard University’s Kennedy School of
Government, and at our annual Capitol Hill Intern Briefing, where our panel of
speakers address student summer interns from across the country. FFL speakers
inspire our courageous pro-life students.

One of our dedicated summer interns put it this way: “Feminists for Life articulates
what I thought and never knew how to say. It prepares me to talk to my peers.”

Even more remarkably, FFL speakers draw crowds of pro-choice students! And isn’t
that who we really need to reach?

Remember, these students have never known a day without abortion. They may show
up suspicious or even combative-like the line of protesters that greeted Serrin
in a hallway outside the lecture forum at Berkeley. (She shook the hand of every
one of those protesters as she went through the crowd.)

But you can see the wheels turning when they hear FFL’s uniquely pro-woman approach,
and they inevitably agree with FFL on many points, even to the point of offering
to work on addressing the unmet needs of women. They actually begin to reconsider
abortion!

Remember the first time you heard the name “Feminists for Life” and understood
our unique capacity to reach those at highest risk? Help us pass it on.

As a father, as a feminist, as an alum, I know that with your help, FFL is uniquely
positioned to succeed. Patrick A. O'Kane

Immediate Past Chair of the Board
