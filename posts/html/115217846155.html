What Are You Doing On Campus For SAAM?

Hey, Dry,

April is Sexual Assault Awareness Month (SAAM), and we want you and your group to get involved, raise awareness, and create action on campus!

Sexual Assault Awareness Month occurs each year in April, and this year’s national SAAM campaign is specifically focused on the campus sexual assault epidemic.There’s a lot you can do on campus to spread the word, get people galvanized for change, and support survivors - all before the semester ends. On our blog, we’ve outlined five ways to get active this month, [ http://feministcampus.org/its-saam2015-here-are-four-ways-to-get-involved/ ] but we’ve got a special opportunity for campus leaders like you. We’ll be negotiating discounts for interested students who want to screen “The Hunting Ground” on campus this April!

“The Hunting Ground,” [ http://www.thehuntinggroundfilm.com/ ] a new documentary from the filmmakers behind “The Invisible War,” on your very own campus! “The Hunting Ground” explores the reality of sexual assault on campus - and the intimidating mess of policies and practices survivors must navigate as they seek justice. Check out the trailer below, and *if you’re interested in hosting a screening, **email us [ mailto:campusteam@feminist.org?subject=%22The%20Hunting%20Ground%22%20campus%20screening ]** by 12 PM EST on Friday, April 3 - we’ll negotiate a discount for you.*
