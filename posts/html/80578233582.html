The ACA birth control benefit requires insurance companies to cover all FDA-approved contraceptives as part of preventive care, meaning millions of women can now access birth control without having to pay copays, coinsurance, or deductibles. But tomorrow, the Court will hear from two for-profit companies that claim they should not have to follow the law because they disagree with some forms of contraception.
