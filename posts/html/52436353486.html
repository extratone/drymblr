CDC: More adults need to get vaccinated
An increase in adult vaccinations is needed to help prevent the spread of disease, according to a report by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).

The report calls for a “substantial increase” in adult vaccinations to help prevent illness and to reduce the risk of spreading diseases, including pneumonia, diphtheria, hepatitis, and whooping cough.

What you should know
“Far too few adults are getting vaccinated against important diseases, and we need to do more,“ said Dr. Howard Koh, an assistant secretary for the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.

Adults who do not get vaccinated put others, including elderly and children at risk. In 2012, for example, four out of five babies who contracted whooping cough caught it from a family member or visitor to their home.

The CDC recommends vaccines for adults based on a person’s age and overall health. Some vaccines, such as flu shots, are important for everyone.
